EBAC Rock Climbing Club
Club Info
  • The goal of the climbing club is to provide a safe and friendly atmosphere to learn how to climb outside.
  • Now that the club has grown to 40 people, it is easy to meet up at nearby climbing gyms with other members, or to set up a day of outdoor climbing at one of the local crags.
  • During the climbing season, the club meets weekly to climb outside.
  • Some gear is available for all club members to use:
    • harness
    • climbing shoes
    • helmet
    • belay device
    • discounted $12 passes to Rock Spot
    • discounted $12 passed to Central Rock Gym Warwick & Glastonbury
  • More experienced climbers teach newer climbers skills such as belaying, setting anchors, and rappelling. Climbers looking for more can also learn how to lead climb.
  • Dues are $25/year
  • Contact: For more information or to be added to the email list, contact Gabe Schwartz, gschwart@gdeb.com
Climbing Destinations
    Lantern Hill is a small climbing wall a stone's throw from EB North Stonington
    Ragged Mountain is in central CT and has about 90 routes. It has both Trad and Toprope routes available.
    Beach Pond is in Exeter RI. A small crag with a handful of short bolted routes.
    Ross Pond is in Killingly CT. There are three main walls of varying height.
    There are many other crags in the area, especially in central CT, but the above ones are the ones we have climbed at as a group.
    Two nearby gyms are Rock Spot in Peacedale, RI and Central Rock Gym in Warwick, RI or Glastonbury, CT. The club has $12 passes available for both gyms for club members.
