EBAC Rock Climbing
EBAC Club Info
  • The goal of the climbing club is to provide a safe and friendly atmosphere to learn how to climb outside.
  • The club has about 40 participants, so it is easy to get a group together to meet up at nearby climbing gyms, or to set up a day of outdoor climbing at one of the local crags.
  • During the climbing season, the club meets weekly to climb outside.
  • Some gear is available for all club members to use:
    • harness
    • climbing shoes
    • helmet
    • belay device
    • discounted $12 passes to Rock Spot
    • discounted $12 passed to Central Rock Gym Warwick & Glastonbury
  • More experienced climbers teach newer climbers skills such as belaying, setting anchors, and rappelling. Climbers looking for more can also learn how to lead climb.
  • Dues are $25/year
  • Contact: For more information or to be added to the email list, contact Gabe Schwartz, gschwart@gdeb.com
Local Climbing Locations
OUTDOORS
    Lantern Hill, North Stonington is a short hike near EB's Eagle Park facility.
    Ragged Mountain, central CT has about 90 routes. It has both Trad and Toprope routes available.
    Beach Pond, Exeter RI is a small crag with a handful of short bolted routes.
    Ross Pond, Killingly CT has three main walls of varying height.
    There are many other crags in the area, especially in central CT, but the above ones are the ones the EBAC climbs as a group.
INDOORS
    Rock Spot (Peacedale, RI)
    Central Rock Gym (Warwick, RI / Glastonbury, CT)
    The club has $12 passes available for both gyms for club members.
